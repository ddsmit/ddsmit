My name id David Douglas Smit. I am a husband, a father, a Mechanical Engineer, a (sort of) Musician and a aspiring developer. 

I've been maried for almost 7 years to my wonderful wife [Yolanda](https://www.courageousengineer.com/about/1). We met while working at John Deere in the supplier quality group. I was drawn in by how she carried herself, her beauty, and her intelligence, and I am still drawn in by those qualities today (I am a very lucky man). You should definately check out her content at [Courageous Engineer](https://www.courageousengineer.com), she has some amazing stories to tell. 

We also have (2) wonderful kids. Kent is currently attending my alma-mater [Iowa State](https://www.iastate.edu) for Software Engineering. It's great to have someone in the house who is in the thick of programming to talk about what we're learning and to bounce ideas off of. We also have a 5 year old daughter named Cydney. She is literally a clone of her mother, strong, smart, and sweet. She loves playing card games (and quite often beats us) and has been teaching herself piano.  

From a young age, I always loved making and building things. I learned BASIC using [Interplay's Learn to Program Basic](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uBYz9syhNAA) because I loved video games and wanted to make my own. I used LTPB to program a Tomagatchi and Pong clone, but unfortunately, I lost the source code :).

I also had a large collection of [Technic Legos](https://www.lego.com/en-us/themes/technic), including one of the first generations of [Mindstorms](https://education.lego.com/en-us/products/lego-mindstorms-education-ev3-core-set/5003400?gclid=EAIaIQobChMI59ixwa2Q6gIVipOzCh1FdgNnEAAYASAAEgIgNPD_BwE&ef_id=EAIaIQobChMI59ixwa2Q6gIVipOzCh1FdgNnEAAYASAAEgIgNPD_BwE:G:s&s_kwcid=AL!790!3!361643866740!e!!g!!lego%20mindstorms) kits. I loved building gearboxes, articulated suspensions (because monster trucks are amazing), and autonomous vehicles. 

As I grew older, my interest shifted more towards hobby class RC cars such as the [Traxxas 4-tec](https://traxxas.com/products/models/electric/4-tec-2-vxl-chassis) (although this version is much nicer than the one I had). I mowed dozens of lawns so I could afford to modify and maintain my fleet for both backyard bashing and racing.

I grew my love of mechanical engineering at [Iowa State](https://www.iastate.edu), where I earned my Bachelor's Degree in Mechanical Engineering. Since then, I've worked at [John Deere](https://www.deere.com/en/) as a MFG Engineer for drivetrains on the large, 4 wheel drive tractors and at [GKN](https://www.gknautomotive.com/) as both a MFG Engineer and Product Development Engineer for Hydraulic Clutch Systems. 

In my most recent role, I started learning [Python](https://www.python.org/) to handle the multiple (and various) data sources I needed to analyze. [Python](https://www.python.org/) has allowed me to automate large chunks of my work as well as improved the incites I've been able to get from the data. Having seen how much more effective I could become by using programming as tool, I decided that I wanted to try and bring these tools to more people in the MFG world (excel is super common and often very painful). Over the last couple years, I've developed libraries to try and simplify the most common task that we do (access our quality database, read and analyze press curves) with the hope that [Python](https://www.python.org/)/[Jupyter](https://jupyter.org/) can become the prefered analysis tool. 

Long term, I'm hoping to take my learnings about teaching other engineers and build an affordable/free coding program for school aged children who may not have that opportunity otherwise. 

If you have any comments or questions, feel free to hit me up on [Twitter](https://twitter.com/davidouglasmit) or send me an email (<david.d.smit@gmail.com>). Happy engineering!
